Get mobile insight into your personal genetics. Well Baby Baby is a beautiful, simple to use mobile application that allows you to understand and explore your personal genetics. With Well Baby Baby running on your Android or iOS mobile device, you'll get up-to-the-minute information about scientific discoveries that apply to your genes. You'll also learn about any hidden genetics that may affect your health. You can share your genetics with friends and family, and find out about any genetic risks you might pass on to a child with your partner. You'll also learn about how your body will respond to certain drugs. You can even make healthy choices to help you live longer and be healthier.

Well Baby Baby is perfect for:

  • Couples who want to maximize their chance of having a healthy baby
  • Couples with family history of genetic conditions
  • Sperm and egg banks, fertility clinics, reproductive endocrinologists and midwives
  • People desiring to conceive with donor eggs or donor sperm
  • Parents arranging marriages
  • People who are interested in the genetic health of their exisiting children
  • Parents with a child who has unexplained health issues, or who have such issues themselves
  • Those who are curious about their genes and how they will impact their health and their future
  • Adult children making medical choices for aged parents
  • People who wish to extend their lifespans by making informed choices based on their genetics

FAQ

How is my data shared?
You can keep your genetic data private, share it publically or share it only with those whom you choose for a specified time. You decide.
What's the difference between genotype, exome and genome?
Genotyping covers a small percentage of select spots in your genes that are known or suspected to cause disease or have other effects. Your exome is a much larger sample of your DNA, almost the whole portion that codes for proteins, and your genome is the whole thing.
How does my DNA get sequenced?
Your DNA is sequenced by a third party lab, such as, 23AndMe, Ambrygen or Illumina. Well Baby Baby uses this data to give you useful information such as your predisposition to a certain disease or whether you are a carrier for a genetic disease you could pass on to your child, and allows you to share it if you wish, for instance with a family member or someone with with whom you are considering having a baby.
How much does it cost?
Genotyping from 23AndMe is $99. Exome and full genome sequencing costs more, currently hundreds and thousands of dollars respectively, but is falling extremely rapidly. For the next year or two most customers are expected to use 23AndMe-style genotyping unless there is a family history of rare disease or you are extremely curious! There is currently no charge for the Well Baby Baby app.
Do I need to have my blood drawn? Do I have to go to a lab?
23AndMe requests about 2 ml (about half a teaspoon) of saliva, which you can collect at home and return in the mail. Other labs may require a blood draw.
Are there recurring costs or just a one time fee?
Your genome doesn't change, so if you have the whole thing sequenced, you don't need to do it again. If you have only part of your genome sequenced (genotyping or exome scan) you may want to have a more complete scan done in the future. Given rapidly drop in sequencing prices you can expect to pay the same amount for the next level up that you payed the first time around. There is one case where you may want a second genome scan done, and that is when you develop cancer. Cancer tumors have a slightly different genome than the person who has them, and sequencing the tumor genome will reveal what gene(s) are different and can suggest which sorts of therapies will be effective in treating the tumor.
What kind of health information can I get from my genes?
While your genome stays the same, science is making new discoveries about our genes weekly (sometimes daily) so with your genotype in hand Well Baby Baby will keep you informed of the discoveries that apply to your genes as they are made. Currently over 200 diagnostic genetic tests are available covering risks for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease, Diabetes, Breast cancer, Tay-Sachs Disease, Crohn's Disease, Phenylketoneuria, Bipolar Disorder and hundreds more.
What sources will be used to find insight about my genetics?
Genetic analysis will be performed based on information found in SNPedia, plus peer reviewed journals such as Nature, Science, PNAS, etc. If you use 23AndMe as your genotyping lab, their reports will be available to you in Well Baby Baby as well.
